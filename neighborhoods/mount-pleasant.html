Mount Pleasant

A storied Lowcountry town across the Cooper River, Mount Pleasant blends historic charm, waterfront beauty, and a lively dining and shopping scene.

A Lowcountry Town with Deep Roots

Mount Pleasant sits on the eastern shore of the Cooper River, directly across from peninsular Charleston, South Carolina. One of the oldest English settlements in the Palmetto State, the town has grown from a quiet colonial outpost into one of the most sought-after addresses in the Charleston metropolitan area — all while holding onto its distinct Lowcountry identity. Sweeping marsh views, centuries-old live oaks draped in Spanish moss, and a salt-tinged breeze set the tone for daily life here.

Architecture and Neighborhoods

Mount Pleasant's built environment tells its history in layers. The Old Village — the town's original core near the waterfront — preserves 18th- and 19th-century cottages, antebellum homes, and bungalows along tree-canopied streets. Moving outward, mid-century ranch homes sit alongside newer planned communities, many framed by tidal creeks and maritime forest. Throughout the town, deep front porches, metal roofs, and board-and-batten siding nod to the regional vernacular that defines the South Carolina Lowcountry.

Waterfront Life and Green Space

Water is never far in Mount Pleasant. The Ravenel Bridge pedestrian and cycling path arcs dramatically over the Cooper River, connecting the town to downtown Charleston and offering panoramic harbor views. Shem Creek — a tidal waterway winding through the heart of town — is lined with waterfront restaurants, kayak launches, and boardwalks where brown pelicans and great blue herons are everyday sights. Palmetto Islands County Park provides trails, a splash zone, and broad open space within the county's expansive park system, while nearby Isle of Palms and Sullivan's Island place ocean beaches just minutes away.

Dining, Shopping, and Local Culture

Shem Creek's restaurant row is a Mount Pleasant landmark, drawing locals and visitors alike for fresh-caught seafood served at dockside tables. Beyond the creek, Coleman Boulevard and the Towne Centre corridor offer a full range of independent eateries, regional chains, and retail. The town's Gullah Geechee heritage, rooted in the surrounding Sea Islands, surfaces in sweetgrass basket weaving — a centuries-old craft tradition — and in cultural celebrations that keep this living history visible and honored. Patriots Point Naval and Maritime Museum, home to the aircraft carrier USS Yorktown, anchors the waterfront and draws history enthusiasts year-round.

Pace and Lifestyle

Life in Mount Pleasant moves at a pace shaped by tides, trails, and the easy rhythm of the coast. Morning walks along the Ravenel Bridge, afternoons on the water, and evenings at a Shem Creek table watching the sun sink behind the Charleston skyline — these are the textures of everyday life here. The town's proximity to downtown Charleston means world-class dining, arts, and culture are a short bridge-crossing away, while Mount Pleasant itself offers the quieter pleasures of a Lowcountry community with genuine historical depth.
